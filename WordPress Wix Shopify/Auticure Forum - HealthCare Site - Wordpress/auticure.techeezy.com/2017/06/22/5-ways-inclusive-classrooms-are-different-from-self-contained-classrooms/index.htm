5 Ways Inclusive Classrooms are Different from Self-Contained Classrooms

Inclusive classrooms differ from self-contained classrooms in several key ways. In inclusive settings, students with disabilities learn alongside their peers in the same general education classroom, often with support from special educators. This environment encourages social interaction, equal participation, and exposure to the standard curriculum with necessary accommodations. In contrast, self-contained classrooms separate students with special needs into specialized environments where teaching is highly individualized and focused on specific goals, such as life skills or adapted academics. While inclusive classrooms promote community and diversity, self-contained classrooms offer more controlled and tailored instruction based on each student’s needs. Inclusive classrooms also foster a sense of belonging and help reduce stigma by allowing all students to learn and grow together. They support peer relationships, improve communication skills, and encourage acceptance of differences from an early age. On the other hand, self-contained classrooms, while offering a structured and focused setting, may limit social opportunities and exposure to diverse learning experiences. Each model has its benefits, but inclusive education aims to create an open, supportive environment where every student can thrive within the broader school community. Another important difference lies in the teaching approach. Inclusive classrooms often use collaborative teaching methods, where general and special education teachers work together to meet the needs of all learners. Lessons are designed to be flexible, using differentiated instruction and assistive tools to support diverse abilities. In self-contained classrooms, teaching is more specialized and individualized, with a focus on specific educational or developmental goals tailored to the smaller group. While inclusive classrooms aim for integration and shared learning experiences, self-contained settings provide more intensive, one-on-one support.
